Since buttons made of natural horns are materials made by cutting the horns as they are made in their natural state, they form an irregular pattern, which looks similar to a color spread like an abstract painting. The unique gloss of the natural horn changes to a natural gloss as time passes as users wear and wash clothes themselves. The intaglio of a button is deeper than the normal button, forming a three-dimensional effect, and showing architectural aesthetics. To make sure this intaglio depth, it was made 1.5 times thicker than a normal button.

While considering the intaglio depth of the button and the black horn's color, reminded a 'Black hole'. So, we started working on installation art that imaged black holes. The remaining stock fabric, black velvet, was dyed with natural indigo to create a variety of colors. Regarding the installation, the location of the dyed fabrics in the space was first planned through a swatch, and a large colorful square was made by sewing the fabric with the fabric, then numerous dummies were fixed as supports at the end edge. Only the center part was hung down to create a depth in space.

As for the film, The clothes being sucked into the black hole are those of mavi karga’s 01 collection, "first button." Several helpers pulled and loosened their clothes together to create a black hole in a zero-gravity universe.
